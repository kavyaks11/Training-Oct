Humans...

Humans are the most abundant and widespread species of primate, characterized by bipedalism and large complex brains.this has enabled the development of advanced tools , culture, language.

Human intelligence

Human intelligence is a mental quality that consists of the abilities to learn from experince, adapt to new situations understand and handle abstract concepts and use knowledge to manipulate one's environment.

Investigations

Much of the excitment amon investigators in the field of intelligence derives from their attempts to determine exactly what intelligence is.different investigators have emphasised different aspects of intelligents in their definitions.

Definitions

For example, in a 1921 symposium the American psycologists Lewis Terman and Edward L.Thorndike differed over the definition of intelligence, Terman stressing the ability to think abstractly and thorndike emphasising learning and the ability to give good response to questions.

Theories

Theories of intelligence , as is the case with most scientific theories, have evolved through a succession of models.For the most influential paradigms have been psycological measurement;cognitive psycology which concerns itself with the process by wich the mind functions;cognitivism and contexualism, a combined approach that studies the interaction between the environment and mental processes.

psycometric theories

psycometric theories have generally sought to understand the structure of intelligence. what from does it take and what are its part,if any? such theories haave generally been based on and established by data obtained from tests of mental abilities.
